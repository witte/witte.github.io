About

I have been working as a composer and sound designer since 2007, collaborating with companies from different countries making music and sound design for games, videos and events.

I have always been interested in technology, started programming at 14 and have worked as a software developer for about 4 years before going to college for music at Universidade do Estado de Santa Catarina. In the last years I have been getting more and more interested in the development of music related applications. That has led me to research and study many of the free/open source software available today as well as the Qt framework, and now I am working on a couple ideas of my own that could hopefully also be a meaningful contribution to the ecosystem.

This website is basically a hub linking to my production related to the interest in both music and software.

Software

  • webArdour

    webArdour is an application that allows users to set their own cue volumes through their web browsers in an Ardour session.




    https://github.com/witte/webArdour

  • Pomodoro

    There's a Dial component in QML that is almost what a pan knob in a DAW should be. I couldn't find any other one to fulfill this function and wanted to understand a little better how the C++ side of the QML components work, so I made this little app which is kind of an excuse for the "Pie" and "PieSlice" components to be used. I don't really know if the Pomodoro technique improves my productivity, but it sure reminds me of not staying in front of the computer for 2 hours straight.


    https://github.com/witte/Pomodoro

  • Mixr

    My first attempt at writing a Qt/QML based application. After using many of the JACK patchbay apps I thought that, in all of them, the process of connecting the audio/midi inputs/outpus between applications was slow and tedious, and could be improved if we had automated ways of doing so. Mixr would be an attempt at that, but I've been thinking of doing things a little different now.


    https://github.com/witte/Mixr
